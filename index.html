THE BOY WHO LIES?

Ministry Denies All Claims of Dark Lord's Return

In what is now being referred to as “The Greatest Hoax of the Century” , fifth-year Hogwarts student Harry Potter continues to insist that He-Who-Must-Not-Be-Named has returned. Despite multiple Ministry statements assuring the wizarding public that “There is Nothing!” to fear.

Hogwarts insiders reveal that Harry Potter's behavior has grown noticeably unstable, with the student allegedly experiencing visions of Dark events and clandestine Death Eater assemblies. Despite these alarming assertions, the Ministry maintains that no such occurrences have been verified and firmly dismisses any notion of You-Know-Who's return or the revival of his supporters.

Hogwarts in Turmoil

Insiders reveal that Potter's behavior has caused serious disruptions within Hogwarts, with students and staff divided over his claims. High Inquisitor Dolores Umbridge has taken firm steps to restore order, implementing stricter discipline policies and conducting daily inspections to curb what she describes as “hysteria-fueled nonsense.”

Several professors, however, are rumored to be sympathetic to Potter's cause, further complicating the already tense atmosphere within the school walls.

THERE IS NOTHING!

Ministry Statement Reaffirms Magical Britain is Safe The Ministry of Magic has issued its strongest denial to date, reaffirming that the Wizarding World remains safe and stable. Minister Cornelius Fudge addressed the press this morning, stating: "Rumors, no matter how loudly repeated, do not make reality. We urge the public to place their trust in official sources, not in the fevered imagination of a fame-hungry schoolboy." The statement was backed by a full Wizengamot declaration ensuring “no credible threat” exists to the security of the magical community.

Public Reaction: Confusion, Outrage, and Rising Fear

The Wizarding World has been thrown into turmoil as news of Harry Potter's claims spread across magical Britain. In homes, shops, and wizarding workplaces, wizards and witches are grappling with a mixture of disbelief, fear, and suspicion.

While a handful of families—particularly those who supported Dumbledore during the first rise of You-Know-Who—believe that Potter's story must be taken seriously, a growing majority view his allegations with deep skepticism.

"We all admire Harry for what he's survived," says Mildred Strout of Wimbourne. "But this is a serious accusation. If He-Who-Must-Not-Be-Named had returned, the Ministry would be the first to tell us."

Tragedy at Triwizard Tournament Sparks Controversial Statements

By Rita Skeeter, Special Correspondent What should have been a night of celebration at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry turned into tragedy and chaos, as Cedric Diggory, Hogwarts champion and beloved student, was found dead shortly after the conclusion of the Triwizard Tournament's final task.

But even as the school reels from the devastating loss, controversy has ignited over Potter's shocking claim: that You-Know-Who has returned to full power and was responsible for Diggory's death.

Potter, already notorious for his past encounters with You-Know-Who, insists that the Dark Lord “is back, alive and gathering followers.” The statement has sent shockwaves through the wizarding community, but Ministry officials have categorically denied the allegations, calling them “unsubstantiated, hysterical, and dangerous.”
